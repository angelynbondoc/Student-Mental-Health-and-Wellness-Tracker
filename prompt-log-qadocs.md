## Entry 1: Testing the App Features

**Date & Task:** April 23, 2026 - Testing all the main features of the Student Mental Health and Wellness Tracker to make sure everything works correctly before the final release.

**The Prompt:**  
"You are a QA tester for a student mental health app built by NEU students. Help me create step-by-step test cases for these features: Search, Recent Activity, Content Moderation, Content Creation, Community Feed, Post Reactions and Comments, Mood Journal, Post Sharing, Notifications, Direct Messaging, and Inbox. For each feature, tell me what to click, what should happen, and what counts as a pass or fail."

**What the AI produced:**  
The AI gave me a complete list of test cases for all 11 features. Each test case had clear steps on what to do, what the correct result should look like, and what to check if something goes wrong. It also flagged which tests were more important than others, especially ones involving student privacy and account security.

**What I changed, rejected, or improved — and WHY:**  
Some of the tests the AI gave were too technical and hard to follow. I simplified the steps so they were easier to understand and actually do. I also added a few checks the AI missed — like making sure a student's private journal never shows up in the public feed, and checking that anonymous posts stay anonymous even after being shared to another community.

**What I learned or decided:**  
Testing is not just about checking if buttons work. The most important tests are the ones that protect student privacy — making sure no one can see who posted anonymously, no one can read another student's private journal, and no private messages show up in the public feed. These are the tests I will always check first.
