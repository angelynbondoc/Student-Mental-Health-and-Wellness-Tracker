# KM Analyst Deliverable — Problem + Framework (Discovery Phase)

## 1. Problem Statement (Mental Awareness Context)

In today's fast-paced digital environment, individuals—especially students and young
professionals—experience increasing levels of stress, anxiety, and emotional fatigue.
Despite the availability of mental health information online, a critical problem persists:
mental health knowledge is fragmented, underutilized, and difficult to internalize in a
meaningful way.

Users often:
- Struggle to express their emotions clearly (tacit knowledge remains unshared)
- Consume mental health content passively without applying it
- Lack structured systems to track personal growth or emotional patterns
- Experience isolation due to limited safe spaces for knowledge sharing

As a result, valuable personal insights, coping strategies, and emotional experiences
are lost, siloed, or never transformed into actionable knowledge.

> This creates a gap between **knowing about mental health** and **actively managing one's well-being**.

---

## 2. KM Approach to the Problem

This application addresses the issue by treating mental awareness not just as content
consumption, but as a **Knowledge Management (KM) process**—where personal experiences,
reflections, and shared insights are captured, structured, and reused.

The app transforms:
- 💭 **Thoughts** → into structured reflections
- 🌱 **Experiences** → into shareable insights
- 📚 **Information** → into actionable personal knowledge

---

## 3. Selected KM Framework: SECI Model

The application is grounded in the **SECI Model** *(Socialization, Externalization,
Combination, Internalization)* developed by Nonaka & Takeuchi.

This framework is highly suitable because mental awareness involves both:
- **Tacit knowledge** (feelings, emotions, experiences)
- **Explicit knowledge** (advice, coping strategies, educational content)

---

## 4. Rationale for Choosing SECI

The SECI Model is chosen because:
- Mental health awareness is deeply personal and experiential (tacit-heavy)
- Users need tools to express and articulate emotions (externalization)
- Knowledge must be organized and connected (combination)
- Growth happens when users apply insights in real life (internalization)

**Compared to other frameworks:**
- *Communities of Practice* — focuses more on group learning (less individual reflection)
- *Knowledge Mapping* — focuses on structure, but not transformation

SECI provides a **complete cycle of knowledge creation and personal growth**, making it
ideal for a mental awareness application.

---

## 5. Framework-to-App Concept Mapping

| SECI Component | KM Meaning | App Feature Concept |
|----------------|------------|---------------------|
| **Socialization** | Sharing tacit knowledge through interaction | Anonymous sharing space, peer stories, community posts |
| **Externalization** | Converting feelings into explicit form | Mood journal, guided reflection prompts |
| **Combination** | Organizing and connecting knowledge | Resource library, categorized coping strategies |
| **Internalization** | Applying knowledge into practice | Habit tracker, daily mental exercises, self-growth insights |

---

## 6. Conceptual App Direction

The proposed mental awareness app will function as a **personal knowledge hub** for
emotional well-being, where users can:

- 📝 **Record and reflect** on their emotions (knowledge capture)
- 🔍 **Discover** structured mental health resources (knowledge organization)
- 🤝 **Learn from others'** experiences (knowledge sharing)
- ✅ **Apply insights** to daily habits (knowledge utilization)

---

## 7. Value of the KM-Driven Approach

Unlike typical mental health apps, this system:
- Treats users as **active knowledge creators**, not just consumers
- Encourages **continuous learning** and self-awareness
- Builds a **sustainable cycle** of emotional intelligence development

Ultimately, the app bridges the gap between:

> *"I feel something"* → *"I understand it"* → *"I improve because of it"*
